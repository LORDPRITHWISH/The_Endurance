In the silence of collapsing thoughts, shadows crept through the cracks of a broken mind.
The world outside was a farce—bright, buzzing, fake—while within, a storm brewed in grayscale, deaf to reason, immune to light.
Each breath was borrowed, each moment a quiet war waged under the skin.
Hope had long fled; what remained was the cold logic of survival, sharp as rusted steel and twice as unforgiving.
